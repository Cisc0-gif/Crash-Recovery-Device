What Features to Look for in the Best Monitor for Programming?

It's not easy finding the right model especially when programmers have different answers as to what makes the best monitor for programming. Some programmers find that color is more important while others prefer adjustability over other factors. To make things easier for you, we've included some of the most important features that the best monitor should have.

  • Screen Resolution
  • Screen Size
  • Response Time and Refresh Rate
  • Screen Orientation
  • Display Panel

Let's study them in detail:

Screen Resolution

Resolution is an important factor when choosing a monitor for programming; the higher the resolution, the more real estate. High-resolution monitors provide better readability making it easier to read and write codes. The 3 most common resolutions are 1080p, 1440p and 4k.

4k monitors are dominating the market, but we understand that their price may send most people away. Having a monitor with 4 times the pixels of a 1080p monitor means you can fit nearly four times the content on the screen.

Despite their high price tags, they are the great monitors for programming because they reduce eye strain. If you can't afford them, make sure you settle for nothing less than a 1080p monitor.

Screen Size

Screen size is more of a personal preference so it may not affect your programming. Some people prefer a smaller screen size because of their desk space, and others prefer larger screens that equally let them watch movies or play games. You can choose the size you prefer however you need to make sure you don't sacrifice the resolution quality. If you are going for large screen size, say a 27-inch screen, you want to consider a higher resolution.

Response Time and Refresh Rate

Even though these are important factors to consider in the best monitor for programming, you don't have to worry about them much. As a recommendation though, you need to consider a monitor with a response time of at least 5ms; the faster the response time, the better the experience. A decent monitor should also have a refresh rate of at least 60Hz. Anything less than this affects day-to-day use.

Screen Orientation

You'll notice that most monitors on the market have a landscape orientation. Portrait monitors are quite rare but are a great investment to programmers. Should you go for a landscape or portrait mode? Well, this is a matter of preference. However, a monitor in the landscape orientation lets you see more code at once. We'd recommend you have at least one monitor oriented in the portrait mode.

Display Panel

All monitors that are on sale in the market nowadays have LCD panels. Simply put, all screens marketed as TFT, IPS and LED are all basically LCDs, with slight changes.

There are 3 popular LCD panel types; IPS (In-plane Switching), TN (Twisted Nematic) and VA (Virtual Alignment). TN panels have the best refresh rate and response time. They are quite popular in high-end gaming monitors. Unfortunately, these panels do not have the best color accuracy or viewing angles. This means that they are not the best choice for web developers.

VA panels while being less popular have better images than TN panels. IPS panels beat other models if you want the best monitor for programming. They have vibrant colors, good contrast, and the best viewing angles. Despite their high price tags, IPS panels pack features that are more suited to programming.

Why use a Monitor Stand?

Most models come with stands that let you tilt the screen without adjusting the height. This may set you in an uncomfortable working position. For comfort, you want to consider a monitor with an ergonomic stand that lets you fully adjust the screen position.

You can equally invest in a separate stand that allows you the flexibility to adjust the monitor to your preferred comfortable position. A custom arm stand is equally important when you are thinking about a dual or triple monitor setup. Before looking for any type of stand, you need to make sure that the monitor you buy is VESA compatible.

Fortunately, most of the models we've included on our list are VESA compatible. Whether you are looking for a clamp stand or a freestanding model, investing in a stand lets you adjust the orientation of your monitor for maximum performance.

How many Monitors to Choose? Dual Monitor or Wide Screen Arrangement?

A few people are curious whether dual monitor arrangement improves productivity than a widescreen arrangement. Most developers prefer having a second monitor because it comes in handy when reading documentation and testing codes.

A widescreen arrangement gives you seamless working experience without bezels between windows. These models have a display size ranging from 29 to at least 34 inches with resolutions of around 1080p or 1440p. Having an ultra widescreen is a good choice for graphics designers or video game programmers.

Final Thoughts

After comparing all these monitors, we suggest a dual monitor setup for programmers. You could choose two Sceptre E205W for your setup. For Designers and gamers we recommend the Samsung CHG90. Though there no one size fits all approach.

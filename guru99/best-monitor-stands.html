Details
Last Updated: 13 February 2020

Which is one of the main reasons you should consider purchasing a monitor mount. It allows you to adjust the height and angle of the screen for optimum viewing. And also, it declutters your workspace which is a proven way of boosting productivity.

In this post, we will review 7 of the top monitor stands you can get in the market right now. Whether you are a programmer, graphic designer or whichever your intended use is, you are certain to find the perfect match for you.

#1: AmazonBasics Monitor Stand – Best Single Arm Mount

AmazonBasics is a well-known brand in the manufacturing industry, and we can agree their products across various sectors have proved to be exceptional. Their single monitor stand may not be the cheapest, but there is a reason it is our top pick for this particular use case.

Compared to the competition, I found it to have a much superior build which leads me to believe it will be more long-lasting. I also loved how versatile this stand is. For starters, the desk clamp will fit snugly on any desk measuring 0.4” to 2.4”. Then there is the fact that you can mount the larger monitors going up to 32 inches (though even 34 inches seem to be supported). Please ensure that you monitor does not weigh more than 25lbs. It supports both 75 and 100mm Vesa mounts.

This stand also allows more adjustment levels. You can tilt the monitor 5 degrees forward and 70 degrees backward and switch between landscape and portrait modes by rotating the stand through 180 degrees.

I was able to adjust the height by 13 inches although this reduced the heavier the monitor became. Monitors weighing more than 20lbs could only extend up to 8.5 inches.

Pros

  • VESA compatible
  • Relatively easy to setup
  • Established brand name
  • Mounts sizably large monitors.

Cons

  • A bit pricey

#2: HUANUO Monitor Mount – Best Dual Monitor Mount

If you are a programmer or a graphics designer, then you already know that having multiple monitors is one of the recommended ways to increase productivity. But two screen on your desk translates to even less workspace. Which is why I recommend the HUANO dual mount stand.

The mount is only able to successfully hold two 27" monitors with a maximum weight of 17.6 lbs. But what I really loved about it is the gas spring arms. These are far superior to mechanical spring arms and effortlessly sustain the monitors at the set height for longer periods.

Talking of which, the maximum height the monitors can be adjusted to is 13.8 inches. The arms also allow a full circle rotation and tilting angles of between -90 degrees to 85 degrees. I was also able to swivel the monitors to a maximum of 180 degrees. Therefore, regardless of where I was sitting, I still had an excellent viewing angle and could easily switch between landscape and portrait modes.

The mount is equipped with a C-Clamp so unlike the Ergotron WorkFit-S whose base covers the entire desk, this only clamps to a section of the desk. Nevertheless, I think the clamp's width could have been made more than the 2.36” currently supported.

The mount supports both the 75x75mm and 100x100mm mounting patterns and comes with a cable management system to reduce wire clutter on your desk.

  • Allows independent movement of each arm

#3: Ergotron WorkFit-S Stand – Best Mount with Work Surface

The Ergotron WorkFit-S was one of the first stands to embrace the sit-stand work routine and has managed to stay at the top for years.

The mount comes equipped with its own workspace and a keyboard tray. Which was the first thing I found impressive since other similar products combine the two. The workspace is quite stable and can carry a maximum weight of 5 lbs. Which means you can use it to hold a cup of coffee and your phone comfortably.

The maximum height I could hoist the monitor is 22.8 inches, but this went down to 18 inches when I opted to move the monitor and the keyboard tray together. It also surprised me how easily the keyboard tray moves. I used just one hand to adjust it.

The desk clamp is compatible with desk surfaces ranging from 0.47" to 2.4". Nevertheless, I would advise that you use the strongest mounting surface since when you combine the weight of the stand plus the mounted monitors the total load is quite bulky.

The stand can only manage to hold two 22lbs monitors not exceeding 22". Unfortunately, the stand is limited in terms of tilt and swivel degrees. This means that it is only adjustable in terms of height and 360 degrees rotation.

But the good thing is that it is VESA compliant and will fit in just about all monitors. It also includes clips and zip ties to secure the connecting wires resulting in an overall clean workstation.

Which Factors to consider when buying your monitor stand?

  • Number of monitors

This is so obvious that I could just skip it. If you own 3 monitors, then the automatic choice for you is a 3-way mount stand unless you plan to acquire an additional one in which case you can purchase the Quad mount. Coders/developers usually opt for dual monitor. Gamers 3 monitors. System Admins 4 or 6.

  • Adjustability

The standard monitor standard is rigid and only allows minimal movement. Therefore, if you are purchasing a monitor stand it should have a higher level of flexibility. Some important aspects to consider in relation to adjustability include the rotation of the mounting arms around the vertical pole, left and right swivel angle and the maximum backwards and forward tilt possible.

  • Height

This refers to the length of the vertical pole. It is important since it determines the monitor’s position relative to your eye. Your eyes should be on the same level as the monitor display in such a way that you do not strain your neck or back to view it.

  • Weight Capacity

Each stand comes with its own weight specification. To avoid overloading the springs that facilitate movement of the mount arms, make sure the weight of your monitor does not exceed the stand’s maximum load capacity.

  • LCD Size

This factor only applies in multiple monitor setups. Due to the spacing between the arm brackets, it may be impossible to exceed a certain screen size. If the screen exceeds the indicated size then they may not fit. Screen size limitation can also be a way to ensure you do not exceed the maximum weight capacity. This is because the larger a screen is then the more it weighs.

  • VESA Compliance

The accepted standard for how monitors, televisions and flat panel displays should be connected to the mounting stand is what is known as the VESA mount. Basically, it refers to the arrangement of the screws and connecting holes. Make sure that both the mounting stand and monitor are VESA compliant. In case your monitor is non-compliant, like most apple products, you will have to buy a VESA adapter to actualize connection.

  • Type of Mount

Depending on the available desk space and intended use, you will have to choose between a wall mount and a desk mount. The wall mount is sturdier and will support more weight but it offers less adjustability and is not ideal for multiple monitor support. For this, you will have to opt for the desk mount stand. This can either be attached to the desk using a C-clamp or grammot base. The latter involves drilling holes to your desk and securing the stand to the desk using screws.

  • Build Material

The most preferred build material is aluminium or steel although chrome is also acceptable. Some mount stands feature a combination of all of them. Nevertheless, what’s important is that the material is strong enough to withstand the monitor weight. Oh, the material should also be able to boost the aesthetics of your workplace.

  • Cable management

Talking about general aesthetics, don’t you hate it when wires hang around everywhere in your office. I find it exhausting. Which is why your stand of choice should have the necessary mechanisms to hold the wires in a neat manner.

Why use a Monitor Stand?

  • Prevents eye strain and promotes back, neck and shoulder health- Monitor stands are adjustable and can, hence, be set to the perfect height that does not strain your eyes, nor exert unnecessary pressure to your neck, shoulder and back. In case you did not know this already, the monitor should be an arm's length away with the top slanted upwards.
  • Aligns multiple monitors on the same level - If you are using more than one monitor, it can be hard organizing them in a parallel setup. However, by mounting them on a monitor arm, you will be able to play around with the settings until they are perfectly aligned.
  • Increases space on your desk - Instead of having the monitors fill up all your desk space, you can simply clamp the monitor stand at the back of the desk. This is especially true if you are using multiple monitors. One stand can hold up to 6 monitors at a time which would otherwise cost you a lot of space. Alternatively, you can opt for a wall mounted stand thus clearing your desk completely.
  • Promotes cooperation – How many times have you had to call your colleague at the other side of the desk to show them something you are working on. Well, the monitor stand will allow you to easily turn the monitor around without them having to move.
  • Improve productivity – When you put all the above features together, what you end up with is increased productivity. Whether you are gaming, programming, or even tracking stock exchanges, mounting your monitor immediately reduces the hassle involved in your work.

Conclusion – Our top pick

I hope this post has been the mind opener we intended it to be. By now you should already have a clear picture of the mounting stand that is best suited for you. But in case you are undecided, I will give you my personal favourite. Mind you, this is not easy especially considering each mounting stand serves a specific functionality.

But if I had to choose then I would go with the VIVO desk mount stand. It is affordable and does not compromise quality. I also like that it offers a multiple monitor setup and is arranged in such a way that I don’t have to necessarily have 4 monitors to use it. I can start with 2 and expand onwards which makes sense to me.
